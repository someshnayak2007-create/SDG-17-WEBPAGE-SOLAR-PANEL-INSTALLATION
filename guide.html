Solar Panel Installation Guide

This easy installation guide will help you understand how solar panels are safely installed.

1. Choose the Right Location

  • Pick a sunny area with no shade.
  • South-facing roofs give maximum power in India.
  • Ensure the roof is strong enough.

2. Install the Mounting Structure

  • Use high-quality mounting frames.
  • Fix them tightly using stainless steel bolts.
  • Ideal tilt angle: 25°–35°.

3. Fix Solar Panels

  • Place panels carefully on the frame.
  • Tighten clamps properly.

4. Wiring

  • Use MC4 connectors.
  • Connect panels in series/parallel as required.
  • Ensure insulation & safety.

5. Connect the Inverter

  • Connect DC output of panels to inverter.
  • Connect AC output to your home power line.

6. Safety Checks

  • Install proper earthing.
  • Add lightning protection if required.
  • Always wear safety equipment: harnesses, gloves, and eye protection.
  • Work with a partner — never work alone on roofs.
  • Use proper rated tools for electrical work and double-check wiring labels.
  • Keep panels covered or oriented to reduce current while wiring (use manufacturer protective caps on connectors).
  • Follow weather conditions — avoid wet or windy days for rooftop work.

7. Tools

  • Drill and screwdriver set
  • Wrenches and socket set
  • Wire stripper and crimper
  • Multimeter
  • Ladder and roof safety harness
  • Measuring tape and marker

8. Materials

  • Solar panels (appropriate wattage for your needs)
  • Mounting rails and brackets (roof compatible)
  • Inverter (string or micro-inverter)
  • MC4 connectors and cabling
  • Earthing/grounding kit
  • Combiner box and fuses (if required)

9. Maintenance & Troubleshooting

Regular maintenance

  • Clean panels lightly with water every 6–12 months if dusty.
  • Inspect mounts and wiring for corrosion or loose fasteners annually.
  • Check inverter display/logs for warnings; note generation trends.

Common issues

  • Low generation: check for shading, dirty panels, or inverter faults.
  • Tripping breakers: inspect wiring and fuses, consult electrician.
Note: For electrical wiring, it's always safer to consult a certified solar technician.
